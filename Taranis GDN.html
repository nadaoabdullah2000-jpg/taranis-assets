Dear Investor,
June was a difficult month for the Global Data Network, returning -19.4% against +3.5% for the Russell Growth Index and -0.7% for the MSCI World.
A sharp rotation hit tech, which we had flagged last month we moved close to 30% of our equity exposure to cash ahead of it. Even so, we could not fully avoid losses, most notably in the space sector.
The central event was the SpaceX IPO, which drove a "buy the rumour, sell the news" pull of flows out of equities and weighed on space names. We sold most of our US space holdings, kept the European ones, and began building Asian exposure — a theme we explore at length inside.
AI and memory names stayed resilient: SK Hynix (+9%) and Micron (+19%), the latter beating quarterly revenue by 24%, reinforced our view that the AI and memory cycle is far from over, while Nebius (+20%) rose on its Eigen AI acquisition and Nasdaq 100 inclusion.
Space was the main drag, led by Astroscale (-57%) — still up 60% year-to-date — with QPS, OHB, and Innospace also lower. We closed June with more equities as conditions improved following the Iran-US de-escalation and clearer guidance from the new Fed Chairman.
