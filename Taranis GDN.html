Dear Investor,
June was a difficult month for the Global Data Network, returning -19.4% against +3.5% for the Russell Growth Index and -0.7% for the MSCI World Index.
A sharp rotation hit technology stocks , which we had partly anticipated and highlighted in our previous newsletter. We moved close to 30% of our equity exposure to cash ahead of it.
The central event was the SpaceX IPO , which attracted a significant share of growth-stock flows and weighed on other space names. We sold most of our US space holdings , kept the European ones and began building exposure to selected Asian companies a theme we explore in more detail inside.
AI and memory names remained resilient. SK Hynix gained +9% , while Micron rose +19%, reinforcing our view that the AI and memory cycle is far from over. Nebius gained +20% , supported by its Eigen AI acquisition, Nasdaq 100 inclusion and a major investment linked to the UK AI plan.
Space was the main drag, led by Astroscale, (-57%) , although it remains up +60% YTD. QPS declined (-44%) , OHB fell (-32%) and Innospace lost (-30%) .
